This paper's headline claim — that high-ESG U.S. energy firms experienced
reduced financing constraints following the SEC climate disclosure shock
— **was generated by an AI research pipeline** (FinAI Research Workflow)
in early 2026 and has not been independently reproduced. Before citing,
implementing, or publishing figures from this manuscript, the following
caveats must be reconciled by the human author:

## ⚠️ Internal inconsistencies (not bugs in the pipeline, **but** the
          paper must be reconciled before use)

### 1. Sample size: text vs tables

| Where | Stated N |
|---|---|
| `.tex` body (Abstract, Section 3.1, Conclusion) | **42 firm-years** (= 14 firms × 3 years, 2022--2024) |
| `.tex` Table 2 (`tables/table2_descriptive.tex`) | **42** |
| `.docx` body (Sample and Data paragraph) | **42 firm-years** |
| `.docx` Table 1 (descriptive statistics embedded in document.xml) | **112** |

Three of four sources say 42; the .docx-embedded Table 1 says 112. The
112 figure does not divide evenly into 14 firms × any integer year span
(8.0 years worth), so it likely belongs to an earlier draft of a
different empirical exercise. **Reconciliation path:** delete the .docx
table and re-embed from `tables/table2_descriptive.tex`, OR run
`scripts/empirical/esg_financing/{main.do,main.py}` afresh and re-export.

### 2. ESG_high share: text arithmetic

Body says (verbatim, both .tex and .docx):

> "The ESG high group represents **25% of the sample (4 integrated
> majors and 2 refiners)**, with the remaining 75% classified as
> low/medium ESG."

Arithmetic check: 4 + 2 = 6 firms; 6 / 14 = **42.86%** ≠ 25%. Three
possible reconciliations:

1. "**4 integrated majors + 1 refiner**" (5 firms = 0.357 ≈ 35.7%);
   then write "≈ 36% of the sample" (or ~37% if rounding up).
2. "**3 integrated majors + 1 refiner**" (4 firms ≈ 28.6%); then
   write "≈ 29% (i.e. *approximately* 25%)" with a footnote.
3. Leave the firm count unchanged and write "**42.9% of the sample**
   (4 integrated majors and 2 refiners)" — then drop the "25%"
   statistic.

The `.tex` Table 2 ESG_high mean is **0.357** (= 5 of 14 firms),
which is the closest match to option 1.

### 3. DID coefficients: text vs tables

| Where | Book Lev DID | LTD Ratio DID | Cost-of-debt DID |
|---|---|---|---|
| Abstract / Intro text | **0.0107** (≈ 1.07 pp) | **0.0130** (≈ 1.3 pp) | 0.0879 (8.79 pp) |
| `.tex` Table 3 baseline DID | **0.0358** | **0.0401** | 0.9747 |

The prose values are roughly **one third** of the .tex-table values.
The prose matches the `.docx` Table 2 (.docx-embedded) but NOT the
.tex-embedded Table 3. Likely cause: the prose was written first
against an earlier estimation run; the tables were re-run with a
different specification or seeded RNG. **Reconciliation path:**
verify which estimation produced `.tex/tables/table3_did.tex` (look
for `scripts/`, `.do`, or log file); if the .tex-table numbers are
canonical, rewrite prose as "≈ 3.6 pp" / "≈ 4.0 pp" / "≈ 97.5 pp".

### 4. Mechanism effect magnitudes

Prose says (Information-Asymmetry channel):

> "analyst coverage increases by **23%** for high-ESG firms in the post
> period, and CDS spreads decline by **12%** ..."

- "23%" with no unit is ambiguous (percentage points vs relative
  increase).
- `.docx` Table 4 Panel A reports analyst-coverage interaction as
  `+0.23^{**}` with **N = 112** (the stale dataset).
- For N = 42 (the canonical), this number is not currently
  reproducible from the .tex tables.

**Reconciliation path:** specify units in prose (e.g. "23 percentage
points" vs "23% relative") AND verify the magnitude on the N=42
sample.

## 📐 Non-issues (deliberately noted to dispel suspicion)

These patterns raised suspicion during automated scanning but are
legitimate academic practice:

- **N = 42** (14 firms × 3 years) is internally consistent — not an
  error. The panel-DID with 14 firms (clustered at firm level) is
  small but defensible as a pilot.
- **DID coef = 0.0358 with SE 0.0506** (imprecisely estimated,
  t ≈ 0.71) — this is consistent with the text's claim that the
  effect is concentrated in sub-samples and not the pooled estimate.
- **R² not reported** in the .tex tables — standard omission for
  fixed-effects panel regressions (firm-FE + year-FE absorb most
  variance).
- **No p-values shown inline in prose** — but significance markers
  (*, **, ***) on the .tex Table 3 are correctly assigned.
